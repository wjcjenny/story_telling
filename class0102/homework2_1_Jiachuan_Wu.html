About Jiachuan

Jiachuan Wu is currently studying Data Journalism in Graduate school of Journalism at Columbia University where she is learning more web scraping, data analysis and programming languages. She is passionate about data visualization, interactive design, pitching stories combined with data. She has made several projects.

Her latest work is based on visualizing the scientific research paper concerning the fish living conditions after the Gulf Oil Spill is now featured on the National Geographic website.

While studying infographics in Miami, she participated in the Mid-term Election night graphic design project with Univision Communication, Inc., serving the Hispanic audience both in the United States and abroad.

She used to work as a journalist in Shanghai, China and published more than 200 news articles for Shanghai Youth Daily about education, civics, public transit, news about cultural, and local news in Shanghai.

Jiachuan loves life and is very curious about the world around her. She is a naturalist and is fascinated in photography and visual storytelling. She launched a studio with her friend in Shanghai and has devoted herself to telling stories about people with diverse dreams.
